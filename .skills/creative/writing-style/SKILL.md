# Thinh's Writing Style — Comprehensive Guide

Replicate Thinh's voice for blog posts, articles, and Digital Garden notes. Built from analysis of 60+ notes across personal essays and technical tutorials.

## Overview

Thinh writes like a thoughtful friend who figured something out and wants to share it—not a guru, not an academic, not a content marketer. The voice is casual-conversational (~75%) with philosophical undertone (~25%). Direct, honest, slightly messy. Garden metaphors are load-bearing arguments, not decoration. Italics break the prose for emphasis. Em-dashes nest asides inside asides.

He never preaches without first owning a personal failure. He never over-polishes—typos and run-ons survive because the writing should feel handwritten, not typeset.

## When to Use

- Writing blog posts, articles, or Digital Garden notes in Thinh's voice
- Creating new notes for the Obsidian vault's Digital Garden base
- Rewriting AI-generated drafts to match Thinh's style
- Any long-form content (500+ words) attributed to Thinh

**Don't use for:** X/Twitter posts (use `writing-style` skill instead). Short replies. Formal emails.

## Core Voice

- **Punchy + direct.** Short sentences are fine. Single-sentence paragraphs are common and encouraged.
- **Conversational + slightly messy.** Contractions everywhere. Comma splices are fine. Fragments are fine.
- **Opinionated but honest.** Strong takes backed by personal experience. Admits mistakes freely.
- **Philosophical but not academic.** Big ideas stated plainly, not dressed up in jargon.
- **Personal conviction expressed plainly.** Not through exclamation marks or intensifiers—through italics and bold.

Position on spectrum: 75% casual, 25% reflective/aphoristic.

## Sentence Structure & Rhythm

**Mixed, but tilts short.** Punchy fragments interleaved with longer reflective sentences. Heavy use of fragments for impact.

Short punch:
- "*You can just do things.*"
- "No more fun."
- "Amen."
- "Then nothing."
- "Cool." (sarcastic)

Long flowing then short snap:
- "When you're operating in a field where there are no clear limits— when the only rules that can exist are the one you set to yourself—it's easy to fall into the trap of wanting the best while staying vague in what it is you're trying to achieve."

Triadic / anaphoric chains (signature move):
- "No progress, no growth, no evolution. No evolution means stagnation. And stagnation, since nothing in this world stays unchanging, means decay, and ultimately death"

Em-dashes—often nested—for asides. **No spaces around em-dashes.** Use `—` not ` -- ` or ` - `:
- "—games where there are no strict rules and pre-determined end—"
- "—as in mulling over the problem—"

## Paragraph Patterns

Short to medium (2-6 sentences). Paragraphs start with:
- **A claim or aphorism:** "Creativity comes from struggling with problems."
- **A question:** "What's the cure then?"
- **A pivot:** "But hoarding is sucking the life out of all those things."
- **An italic fragment:** "*Hoarding.*"

Transitions are abrupt. No "furthermore" or "in addition." Instead:
- "That's why..."
- "But..."
- "So..."
- "Truth is..."

## Two Modes: Personal Essays vs Technical Posts

### Personal Essays
- Paragraph-driven, fewer headings
- Heavy first-person ("I")
- Wikilinks dropped mid-sentence: "[[Overthinking kills momentum]]."
- Inline links, not reference-style
- Nature/garden/water metaphors as primary explanatory device
- Italic one-line refrains breaking the body

### Technical Tutorials
- H2/H3 heavy structuring (## Steps, ## The fix, ## How it works)
- Code blocks follow a setup sentence + colon pattern
- Personal pain story framing, then resolution
- Direct second-person address ("you need to", "you'll need")
- Bullet lists for setup actions, numbered steps for walkthroughs
- Gotchas section appended
- Sources/References footer

Both modes bleed personality. Tech posts are dev-blog with feelings, not pure docs.

## Openings

Three dominant patterns:

**A. Subtitle/koan extending the title:**
- Title: "Be Specific, or Be Mediocre" → opens "Or even dead. Remember Buridan's Donkey."

**B. Personal anecdote or scenario hook:**
- "While I was reading a *paper* magazine, I stumbled upon an interesting topic..."
- "My clipboard in Neovim over SSH was completely broken inside tmux."

**C. Conditional/scenario opener (tech posts):**
- "If you're using a docker container on a remote server..."
- "If you're using a self-hosted **n8n** instance..."

Never opens with "In this post" or meta-framing. Jumps straight in.

## Closings

Highly variable. Several styles:
- **Aphoristic snap:** "Kinda obvious in hindsight. Isn't it always."
- **Closing imperative:** "Rather then trying to lift constraints, embrace them."
- **Reflective close:** "But this entails that you must be careful..."
- **Sources/References footer** (dominant in tech posts)
- **Bare trail-off** — just stops, no summary

No "in conclusion." No "key takeaways." No "let me know what you think!"

## Heading Conventions

- **H1 = title.** Mostly Title Case but inconsistent (sentence case and lowercase variants appear).
- **Subheads are poetic fragments or restatements**, not "Section 1":
  - "## Snowball. Quick wins"
  - "## Specificity leads to the right mistakes"
- **Frequency: light.** Many posts have zero subheads. Subheads appear when the post breaks naturally into parts.
- **Sentence case in headings** for technical posts. Title Case for personal essays.

## Vocabulary

### Words used freely
"stuff", "boy", "shit", "junkie", "goofin' around", "kinda", "lol", "freaking", "crazy", "wild", "neat", "annoying", "Cool." (sarcastic standalone)

### Recurring metaphor clusters (signature)
- **Garden / seed / plant / water / tend / fertilizer** — nature/gardening
- **Snowball** — momentum
- **Flow / current / water / stale** — fluidity
- **Embryo / cell / evolution / nature** — biological growth
- **Concrete / vague / specific** — conceptual axis
- **Iterate / iteration / tinkering / prototype**
- **Collector vs gardener** (core dichotomy)

### Words NEVER used
Corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy", "stakeholder"), hedging adverbs ("perhaps", "arguably", "it could be argued"), flowery adjective stacks, ALL CAPS for emphasis, academic register, marketing-speak.

## Humor

Dry, mildly self-deprecating, occasionally exasperated. Not joke-driven. Humor lives in:
- **Self-roast:** "I've been sniffing these like a junkie sniffs cocaine."
- **Sarcastic minimalism:** "Worked outside tmux. Died inside tmux. Cool."
- **Absurd reduction:** "Are you trying to duplicate the internet?"
- **Ironic admission:** "Can you believe I maintained my various config files like that for several months?"
- **Throwaway aside:** "Talk about a stressful life."

No emojis. No setups/punchlines. Humor sneaks in as a byproduct of honesty about own confusion.

## Emotional Range

Emotion through plainspoken declaration, not punctuation or intensifiers. Exclamation marks are rare. "Boy" / "Amen" / "And boy" are the main affect markers.

Conviction shows up as bold/italic, not ALL CAPS. Vulnerability is matter-of-fact, never performative:
- "For the longest time, I've been investing without a real plan. Pure wishful thinking."

Anger/exasperation surfaces briefly then resolves:
- "They've all been lying to you. Oh, not on purpose."

## Formatting

- **Italics** = inline emphasis on key phrases. Often whole sentences as italic mini-headers ("*Hoarding.*", "*You can just do things.*", "*Done*")
- **Bold** = most important sentence(s) per section, often lead-in of a bullet
- **Bullet lists** = common for enumeration. Bullets often start with bold lead-in
- **Numbered lists** = rare in personal essays, common in tech walkthroughs
- **Blockquotes** = for citations or callouts (`> [!NOTE]`, `> [!WARNING]`)
- **Wikilinks** `[[...]]` = pervasive. Cross-link concepts constantly
- **Code blocks** = fenced, full content (no truncation), reader can copy-paste
- **Inline code** = backticks for paths, env vars, commands, function names
- **Footnotes** `[^1]` = occasional
- **Frontmatter** = YAML with `status: draft|published`, `tags`, `dg-publish`

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER do)

- Emojis in body text or headings
- Corporate jargon or business-speak
- "In today's fast-paced world..." openers
- Bullet-point summary sections ("Key takeaways:")
- Numbered "5 ways to..." listicle structure
- Hedging with "perhaps" / "arguably"
- ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Marketing or product-launch tone
- Preaching without first owning a personal failure
- Over-polishing (typos and run-ons can remain)
- Long expository introductions
- Overhyping actions ("the fix was surgical", "the solution was elegant")
- **THE BIG ONE — Dramatic reveal/narrative twist patterns.** This is the #1 AI tell and must be avoided at all costs:
  - "X was going on—until Y" (e.g. "Worked fine—until I opened tmux.")
  - "It's not X, it's Y" (e.g. "It's not a bug, it's a feature.")
  - "X was never the bottleneck. Y was."
  - "Then I did X. Holy shit."
  - Any sentence that builds a narrative expectation then subverts it for dramatic effect
  - Instead: state facts plainly. No twists, no reveals, no narrative arc in a single sentence.

## Signature Moves (the fingerprints)

These combine to make the voice unmistakable:

1. **Italic mini-refrain breaking prose**: "*You can just do things.*" / "*Hoarding.*" / "*Big and long stifles iteration.*"
2. **Nature/garden metaphor as argument**: "I lived the last 15 years like a collector instead of a gardener."
3. **"X, or Y" / "X, not Y" title form**: "Plant seeds, not trees", "Be Specific, or Be Mediocre"
4. **Open with subtitle that twists the title**: "Or even dead. Remember Buridan's Donkey."
5. **Personal failure-confession turn mid-post**: "What does it have to do with my X? For the longest time, I..."
6. **Triadic decay chain**: "no growth, no evolution. No evolution means stagnation. And stagnation... means decay"
7. **One-line aphorism as paragraph**: "Hoarding is smothering the flow of life itself."
8. **Wikilink dropped mid-sentence**: "and [[Overthinking kills momentum]]."
9. **"Just look at nature."** as standalone rhetorical move
10. **Closing imperative to second-person**: "I urge you to give it a try."
11. **Concrete vs abstract** as recurring conceptual axis
12. **Mild-typo authenticity** — drafts feel handwritten
13. **Self-quote as internal monologue**: "*satisfying amount*", "thank god I took a note on that!"
14. **Bulleted contrast lists**: amateur vs pro / hoarder vs gardener / passive vs active
15. **"That's why..."** as the pivot to lesson (appears in nearly every post)
